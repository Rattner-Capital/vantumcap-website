Executive Summary

Core Thesis

We acquire small, sub-institutional manufactured housing communities (MHPs) at discounted pricing, aggregate them into scaled portfolios, and exit at institutional valuations—capturing value through structural market inefficiencies that persist in this highly fragmented asset class.

03

03

Why Small Parks Trade at a Discount

Pricing Inefficiency

Small parks (25–50 pads) trade at significant yield premiums to institutional portfolios due to structural factors that create an exploitable market inefficiency.

Operational Complexity

Small parks have higher per-unit management costs and lack economies of scale in operations, maintenance, and administration.

Limited Financing Options

Agency financing (FNMA/FHLMC) requires 50+ pads minimum, forcing small park buyers into expensive bank debt or seller financing.

Restricted Buyer Pool

Institutional buyers cannot deploy capital efficiently below 100+ pads, limiting competition and compressing pricing for small assets.

The Opportunity
By aggregating 6–12 small parks into a 250–400 pad portfolio, we transform sub-institutional assets into an institutional-quality offering.

A.06

Risk Factors & Mitigation

Due Diligence

Market Risk

Interest rate volatility affects cap rates and exit pricing.

Mitigation: Lock in long-term fixed-rate agency debt; target 300+ bps spread to absorb rate movement.

Operational Risk

Small parks require hands-on management; tenant issues can escalate.

Mitigation: Anchor + Satellite model centralizes operations; professional property management overlay.

Regulatory Risk

Rent control legislation or zoning changes could impact operations.

Mitigation: Focus on Midwest markets with limited rent control; proactive resident relations.

Concentration Risk

Geographic clustering creates single-market exposure.

Mitigation: Target 2–3 distinct Midwest markets; diversify across MSA sizes.

Liquidity Risk

3–5 year hold period limits immediate liquidity.

Mitigation: Strong cash-on-cash yields provide income; multiple exit channels (REITs, PE, recap).

Downside Protection

  • • High occupancy (95%+) provides cash flow stability
  • • Affordable housing demand is recession-resistant
  • • Hard asset backing with replacement cost above market value
  • • Multiple value creation levers (NOI growth + cap rate compression)
